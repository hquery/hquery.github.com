An Open-Source Distributed Query Framework for Health Data

  • Enables large-scale aggregate health data queries
  • Preserves patient privacy

What is hQuery?

hQuery is an open-source implementation of a framework for distributed querying of health data. The hQuery Gateway exposes a set of patient records for aggregate queries. The hQuery Composer is used to build a query, distribute it to one or more gateways, and obtain the results. hQuery integrates with certified EHR systems by leveraging their ability to generate HITSP C32 or ASTM CCR patient summaries.

Who are the intended users?

hQuery supports clinical researchers and population health authorities who need to perform custom queries over large patient populations but who lack the facilities or desire to import large patient data sets into their own IT infrastructure. EHR vendors and healthcare providers are free to download, use, and integrate the hQuery software in their systems.

What is the project's objective?

hQuery is designed to support large-scale summary reporting of health data that is distributed over multiple network endpoints. hQuery builds on scalable internet technologies and algorithms to allow use with arbitrarily large data sets using widely available standards-based code libraries.

For more information, contact us at: talk@projecthquery.org
